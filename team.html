Our Team

Meet the passionate people behind Maasai Crafts

Leadership Team

Sarah Mwangi

CEO & Founder

Sarah founded Maasai Crafts in 2018 after witnessing the incredible talent of local artisans during her travels through Narok County. With a background in business administration and a deep passion for cultural preservation, she envisioned a platform that would bridge traditional craftsmanship with modern markets while ensuring fair compensation for artisans.

"I was inspired by the stories my grandmother told me about Maasai culture. When I saw these beautiful crafts being made by hand, I knew I had to help share them with the world. Every piece tells a story, and every purchase supports a family and preserves a tradition."

Education

MBA in Business Administration, University of Nairobi

Experience

8+ years in social enterprise and community development

John Ole Kipas

Operations Manager

Born and raised in Narok, John brings deep cultural knowledge and community connections to our operations. He ensures our partnerships with local communities are authentic and mutually beneficial, overseeing logistics and artisan relations.

"Growing up in a Maasai family, I learned the value of community and tradition. Now I help other families preserve their culture while building sustainable livelihoods."

Grace Nkatha

Quality Assurance Manager

Grace ensures every product meets our high standards while maintaining cultural authenticity. With a keen eye for detail and deep understanding of traditional techniques, she works closely with artisans to maintain quality.

"Quality isn't just about perfection—it's about preserving the soul of each piece while ensuring it meets our customers' expectations."

Master Artisans

Mary Ole Nkuruna
Master Beadwork Artisan

Mary learned beadwork from her grandmother at age 8 and has been perfecting her craft for over 30 years. She specializes in traditional bracelets and necklaces, teaching her techniques to younger artisans.

Specialties:
  • Traditional beaded bracelets
  • Statement necklaces
  • Cultural teaching

"Every bead I place carries the wisdom of my ancestors."

James Ole Saitoti
Master Leather Craftsman

James creates beautiful leather sandals and bags using traditional Maasai techniques. His work combines functionality with cultural significance, ensuring each piece tells a story of heritage.

Specialties:
  • Leather sandals
  • Handbags and pouches
  • Traditional leatherwork

"Leather work connects me to the land and our cattle-keeping traditions."

Grace Naserian
Jewelry Design Specialist

Grace creates stunning necklaces and earrings that celebrate the vibrant colors of Maasai culture. Her designs blend traditional patterns with contemporary style, making them perfect for modern wear.

Specialties:
  • Statement necklaces
  • Beaded earrings
  • Contemporary designs

"I love creating pieces that honor tradition while embracing modern style."

Peter Ole Lenku
Master Wood Carver

Peter carves beautiful wooden bowls and decorative items using traditional Maasai patterns. His work reflects the deep connection between the Maasai people and nature, with each piece telling a story.

Specialties:
  • Decorative bowls
  • Wooden sculptures
  • Traditional patterns

"Wood speaks to me—it tells stories of the trees and the land."

Sarah Naserian
Textile Art Specialist

Sarah creates beautiful wall hangings and textiles using traditional weaving techniques. Her work preserves the ancient art of Maasai textile making while creating functional and decorative pieces.

Specialties:
  • Wall hangings
  • Traditional textiles
  • Weaving techniques

"Weaving is like telling stories with threads and colors."

David Ole Kipas
Pattern Design Master

David is known for his intricate beadwork patterns that tell stories of Maasai life, from cattle herding to traditional ceremonies. His work is highly sought after for its attention to detail and cultural authenticity.

Specialties:
  • Complex beadwork patterns

"Each pattern I create tells a story of our people and our way of life."
