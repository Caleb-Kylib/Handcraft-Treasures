Featured Story: Mary from Narok

"Beadwork is not just a craft for meâ€”it's my connection to my ancestors and my way of preserving our culture for future generations."

Mary learned the art of Maasai beadwork from her grandmother when she was just 8 years old. Now, at 45, she has been creating these beautiful pieces for over 30 years, teaching her own daughters the traditional techniques. Each piece she makes carries the stories and wisdom of her ancestors.

"When I make these bracelets, I feel connected to my culture and my family. Every bead I place tells a story that has been passed down through generations. I'm proud to share this beautiful tradition with the world."
